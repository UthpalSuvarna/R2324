Free Open Source Software

What is FOSS?

Free and Open-Source Software,in short,FOSS,allows it's users to edit,modify or reuse the software's source code.Hence,the developers have the ability to modify the programme and improve it's functionality.The term "free" indicates that there are no constraints on copyrights and the term "open source" indicates that the software is in it's project form, which enables easy development of software from expert developers worldwide.This software is also referred to as free/libre open source softwaare(FLOSS) or free/open source software(F/OSS).

History of FOSS

The origins of FOSS go back to the 1950s. When the hardware was purchased, there used to be no additional charges for bundled software and the source code would also be available in order to fix possible bugs in the software.It was actually a common practice back then for users with the freedom to customize the code.At that time, primarily academicians and researchers in the industry were the collaborators to develop such software.The term Open Source was not there yet. Instead, the term that was popular at that time was “Public Domain Software”. As of today, ideologically, both are very much different in nature even though they may sound similar.Back in 1955, some users of the IBM 701 computer system from Los Angeles, voluntarily founded a group called SHARE. The “SHARE Program Library Agency” (SPLA) distributed information and software through magnetic tapes.Technical information shared, was about programming languages, operating systems, database systems, and user experiences for enterprise users of small, medium, and large-scale IBM computers.The initiative that is now more than 60 years old, continues to follow its goals quite actively. SHARE has its upcoming event coming up as SHARE Phoenix 2019.

GNU

GNU is an operating system that is free software—that is, it respects users' freedom. The GNU operating system consists of GNU packages (programs specifically released by the GNU Project) as well as free software released by third parties. The development of GNU made it possible to use a computer without software that would trample your freedom.GNU is a Unix-like operating system. That means it is a collection of many programs: applications, libraries, developer tools, even games. The development of GNU, started in January 1984, is known as the GNU Project. Many of the programs in GNU are released under the auspices of the GNU Project; those we call GNU packages.The name “GNU” is a recursive acronym for “GNU's Not Unix.” “GNU” is pronounced g'noo, as one syllable, like saying “grew” but replacing the r with n.The program in a Unix-like system that allocates machine resources and talks to the hardware is called the “kernel.” GNU is typically used with a kernel called Linux. This combination is the GNU/Linux operating system. GNU/Linux is used by millions, though many call it “Linux” by mistake.GNU's own kernel, the GNU Hurd, was started in 1990 (before Linux was started). Volunteers continue developing the Hurd because it is an interesting technical project.

Advantages of FOSS

Free and open software, also known as FOSS, has numerous advantages. First, FOSS can be freely downloaded, installed, and used by anyone, without the need to pay for licenses or subscriptions. This makes it accessible to everyone, regardless of their financial means. Additionally, FOSS can be modified and customized by the user, allowing for greater flexibility and adaptability. FOSS also tends to be more secure than proprietary software, as the code is open for anyone to review and identify and fix vulnerabilities. Finally, FOSS fosters a community of collaboration and knowledge-sharing, which can lead to more rapid innovation and problem-solving. Overall, the advantages of free and open software make it an attractive and viable alternative to proprietary software for individuals, businesses, and organizations.
